MAXINE'S WORLD — VILLAGE HOME STORYBOOK WAYFINDING SPEC v1.5

AUTHORITY
This file overrides the v0.14.0 hanging-bamboo-sign implementation for Village Home. Preserve the illustrated village background and subject architecture. Replace only the ornate bamboo UI surfaces and their layout code.

WHY v0.14.0 FAILS
1. Seven-piece bamboo assemblies create visual noise at small sizes.
2. Rattan corner ornaments resemble resize handles and compete with text.
3. Thick rails and hanging tabs consume space without communicating state.
4. The repeated frame motif turns the village into a labeled diagram rather than an explorable game world.
5. The Daily Quest is too small internally but overly decorated externally.
6. Tiny brown details have insufficient contrast against the rich background.
7. The implementation depends on multiple raster pieces and exact layering, increasing failure risk for a weaker LLM.

NEW DIRECTION
Use quiet storybook wayfinding: a warm painted-paper surface, soft 16dp radius, 1dp blue-ink outline, one 6dp subject-color marker, one subject icon, native text, and a 4dp soft shadow. Filipino craft influence comes from the warm woven-paper tone and illustrated village itself—not literal bamboo around every control.

NON-NEGOTIABLE RULES
* Do not render bamboo rails, rattan corners, ropes, medallions, hanging tabs, wood posts, or NinePatch borders.
* Do not use pure-white Material cards.
* Do not bake text, progress, locks, or TODAY into images.
* Do not restore a destination grid on expanded landscape.
* Do not omit Filipino, Philippine History, or GMRC.
* Do not enable a control with an empty callback.
* Do not route a locked subject to unrelated lesson content.
* Do not change the village background in this iteration.

PROFILE
Keep Hi, Maxine, level, XP numbers, and one progress bar inside 270x90dp. Use the approved Maxine avatar when available. Never render a blank extension beneath the content. No app version or build text.

DAILY QUEST
Place directly below Profile with 10dp gap. Show one icon, Daily Quest heading, one-line objective, compact progress, and a 48dp-minimum Continue button. It must never span the screen.

REWARDS
Use three compact warm-paper chips at top-right for streak, stars, and coins. Do not add bamboo frames. Icons carry color; the surfaces remain quiet.

BOTTOM NAVIGATION
Use one low-profile warm-paper navigation bar. No bamboo border. Maintain four items: Profile, Achievements, Backpack, Parents. Parent opens a parent gate. Every enabled item must navigate.

ACCESSIBILITY
* Each destination is a single semantic node.
* Announce destination, subject, percentage, recommendation/completion/lock state, and button role.
* Minimum target 48x48dp; supplied destination target is 184x72dp.
* Background and endemic animals are decorative and normally excluded from TalkBack.
* State must not rely on color alone.
* At 200% font scale, switch to a scrollable compact layout rather than shrinking text.

COMPACT LAYOUT
For width below 840dp, do not use absolute scene anchors. Use an illustrated header, compact profile and quest, then six storybook destination cards in two columns; use one column when available card width would fall below 160dp or at 200% font scale. Keep the same surface style and subject parity.

ASSET CONTRACT
Production assets in this package are Android VectorDrawables only. They have no external dependencies and no alpha/checkerboard risk. The current village_background.webp remains the scene asset. The target screenshot is reference-only and must not be used as a tappable bitmap.

* A destination is larger than another destination.
* Quest is full-width.
* Village coverage by opaque UI exceeds 25%.
* Labels are detached from their buildings.

* White cards dominate the scene.
* Text is baked into artwork.
* Fewer than six subjects appear.
* Enabled controls have no action.
